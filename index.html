Advance Health Informatics

Information throughout human era has been a very important aspect of success, especially in health and medicine. Technology specifially computers are getting more advanced an accessible throughout the world, and is being used for many things such as health informatics. The extracting, managing, utilization of information in health and medicines can significantly improve the much needed quality of medical care and response to public emergencies. They are also ranging from local to global, from individual patiens to sharing data about disease outbreaks for governments and international health organizations. Many things have been done with systems engineering to make information about patients and maintaing them sufficient, but there is much to do to make it optimal in usage.

How can informatics improve the response to public health emergencies?


On a local to global scale, robust health informatic systems could potentially benefit health professionals to detect, track, and prevent both natural health and terrorism emergencies. Biological and chemical warfare can happen anywhere still even in this era, that's why it's important to come up with a solution to these problems. Terrorist have a vast variety of biological and chemical weapons examples being anthrax, ricin, and botulism neurotoxin. Anthrax has been getting a lot of special attention because of the amount of deaths it caused lately.

How to prepare against chemical and biological weapons?

Providing data to give to a informatics system in preparation of terrorists biochemical and chemical attacks involve three main things: Surveillance and detection-- monitoring the early signs of attacks are important such the chang ein air, water, soil, and food; Rapid diagnosis-- needing a system that can analyze and identify the originator of harm as well as track its current real time location and it's effects like spread; Countermeasures-- nimble operations that can develop and mass-produce antidotes, vaccines, or other treatments to counter the effects of the terrorist attacks. This is a major challenge and still in it's early phase. Examples of things that can detect differences is the "artifical noses", kind of like the canine noses but not better. Other toxins and viruses can also be found by using biological detectors, such as ultra-tiny biological "nanopore" devices that send electrical signals when a dangerous molecule is passed through the pore. Just data that can track and identify these information really make it possible to idenitfy biowarfare agents quicky with simple things like blood tests.
